# AeroNova Airlines — Lost, Delayed, and Damaged Baggage

## Overview

AeroNova Airlines understands that baggage issues are among the most stressful
experiences a traveler can face. This document outlines the procedures for reporting
and resolving lost, delayed, and damaged baggage as well as the compensation passengers
are entitled to under AeroNova's baggage liability policy.

## Delayed Baggage

Baggage is considered delayed when it does not arrive on the same flight as the
passenger. This is most commonly caused by tight connections, baggage handling errors,
or late check-in at the origin airport.

If a passenger arrives at the destination and their baggage has not appeared on the
carousel within 30 minutes of the last bag being delivered from the flight, the
passenger should proceed to the AeroNova baggage services desk located in the arrivals
hall before leaving the airport. Passengers who leave the airport without reporting
delayed baggage will experience significantly longer resolution times.

At the baggage services desk the passenger must complete a Property Irregularity Report
(PIR). The PIR number will be used to track the claim and must be referenced in all
subsequent communications. Staff will use the WorldTracer baggage tracking system to
locate the missing bag and provide an estimated delivery timeline.

In most cases delayed baggage is located and delivered within 24 to 48 hours. AeroNova
will arrange delivery of the recovered bag to the passenger's address at no charge.
Passengers are not required to return to the airport to collect delayed baggage.

### Interim Expenses for Delayed Baggage

Passengers whose baggage is delayed by more than 12 hours are entitled to claim
reimbursement for reasonable interim expenses such as essential clothing and toiletries.
Reimbursement limits are as follows:
- Delays of 12 to 24 hours: up to $50 per passenger
- Delays of 24 to 48 hours: up to $100 per passenger
- Delays exceeding 48 hours: up to $150 per passenger

Passengers must retain all receipts and submit them along with their PIR number through
the AeroNova baggage claims portal at www.aeronova.com/baggage-claims or by post to the
AeroNova Baggage Claims department. Reimbursement requests must be submitted within
21 days of the original flight date. Claims submitted after this deadline will not
be processed.

Reimbursement covers only essential items. AeroNova will not reimburse luxury purchases,
electronics, or items unrelated to the immediate travel need. Final reimbursement amounts
are assessed on a case by case basis and AeroNova reserves the right to decline claims
that do not meet the reasonable expenses criteria.

## Lost Baggage

Baggage is officially declared lost if it has not been located within 21 days of the
Property Irregularity Report being filed. Before the 21-day period has elapsed baggage
is treated as delayed and the delayed baggage procedures apply.

Once baggage is declared lost the passenger is entitled to compensation up to the
standard liability limit of $1,500 per passenger. Passengers who declared excess value
at check-in are entitled to compensation up to their declared value subject to the
terms of the excess valuation agreement.

To claim compensation for lost baggage passengers must submit the following documents
through the AeroNova baggage claims portal:
- Copy of the original Property Irregularity Report
- Proof of ownership or purchase receipts for items in the lost bag where available
- Completed lost baggage compensation claim form available on the AeroNova website
- Copy of the boarding pass or booking confirmation

AeroNova will assess the claim within 30 days of receiving all required documentation.
Approved compensation will be paid to the passenger's nominated bank account or as
an AeroNova Travel Credit at the passenger's preference. Travel credits for lost baggage
compensation are valued at 110% of the cash amount as an additional gesture of goodwill.

## Damaged Baggage

Passengers who discover that their baggage has been damaged during transport must report
the damage at the AeroNova baggage services desk before leaving the airport arrivals area.
Claims for damaged baggage will not be accepted after the passenger has left the airport
unless the damage is internal and could not have been discovered immediately upon collection.

Internal damage that is discovered after leaving the airport must be reported within
7 days of the flight date. All damage claims must be submitted with photographic evidence
of the damage and the original PIR or damage report number.

AeroNova's liability for damaged baggage covers repair costs where the bag is repairable.
If the bag is beyond economical repair AeroNova will offer a replacement of equivalent
value or compensation up to the depreciated value of the bag based on its age and
original purchase price. AeroNova does not cover damage to fragile items that were
inadequately packed, pre-existing damage, minor wear and tear such as scratches and
scuffs, or damage to protruding parts such as wheels handles and straps that are
considered normal incidents of air travel.

## High Value and Fragile Items

Passengers are strongly advised not to pack high value items including electronics,
jewelry, cash, important documents, or fragile items in checked baggage. AeroNova's
standard liability does not cover these items in checked baggage unless excess valuation
was declared at check-in.

Fragile items placed in checked baggage are transported at the passenger's own risk.
AeroNova may attach a fragile label to such items at the passenger's request but this
does not guarantee special handling and does not affect AeroNova's liability limitations.

## WorldTracer Baggage Tracking

Passengers can track the status of their delayed or lost baggage online using the
WorldTracer system at www.aeronova.com/track-baggage. The PIR number and last name
are required to access the tracking system. Updates are posted as the bag is located
and processed through the baggage handling network.
