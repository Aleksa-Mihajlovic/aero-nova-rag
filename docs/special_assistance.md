# AeroNova Airlines — Special Assistance

## Overview

AeroNova Airlines is committed to ensuring that all passengers regardless of their
physical or medical condition can travel safely and comfortably. Special assistance
services are available free of charge unless otherwise stated. Passengers requiring
special assistance are strongly encouraged to notify AeroNova at least 48 hours before
departure to ensure all necessary arrangements are in place.

Special assistance can be requested during the booking process, through Manage My
Booking, by calling customer support, or at the airport check-in desk. Requests made
less than 48 hours before departure will be accommodated where possible but cannot
be guaranteed.

## Wheelchair and Mobility Assistance

AeroNova provides wheelchair assistance at all airports it operates from. Passengers
who require a wheelchair from the moment of arrival at the airport through to boarding
should request full wheelchair assistance. Passengers who can walk short distances but
require assistance at the gate or on the jet bridge should request gate wheelchair
assistance only.

Passengers who use their own manual wheelchair may check it free of charge at the
check-in desk or at the gate. The wheelchair will be returned to the jet bridge upon
arrival where possible or at the oversized baggage carousel if jet bridge return is
not available at the destination airport.

Electric and motorized wheelchairs are accepted but must be declared in advance due
to battery handling requirements. Lithium battery powered wheelchairs must have the
battery removed or isolated during transport. AeroNova staff can assist with this
process at the check-in desk. Passengers should contact AeroNova at least 72 hours
before departure when traveling with a motorized wheelchair.

## Passengers with Visual or Hearing Impairments

Passengers who are blind or have low vision will be assisted from the check-in desk
to the aircraft seat by an AeroNova staff member. Safety briefings are available in
audio format and Braille safety cards are available on request.

Passengers who are deaf or hard of hearing should inform AeroNova in advance so that
visual alerts and written communications can be arranged during the flight. AeroNova
staff at the gate will ensure that boarding announcements are communicated directly
to the passenger if requested.

Guide dogs and assistance dogs are accepted in the cabin free of charge on all
AeroNova flights. Documentation confirming the dog's training and certification must
be presented at check-in. The dog must remain on the floor at the passenger's feet
for the duration of the flight and must not occupy a seat.

## Passengers with Medical Conditions

Passengers with medical conditions that may affect their fitness to fly should consult
their doctor before traveling. AeroNova recommends that passengers with recent surgery,
serious cardiovascular conditions, or respiratory conditions obtain medical clearance
before booking.

Passengers who require supplemental oxygen during the flight must notify AeroNova at
least 72 hours before departure. AeroNova provides in-flight oxygen for medical use
at a fee of $80 per flight segment. Personal oxygen concentrators approved by AeroNova
may be brought on board as carry-on items provided they meet the technical requirements
outlined in AeroNova's medical equipment policy.

Passengers with pacemakers or other implanted electronic devices should inform AeroNova
staff at security and at the gate. These passengers will receive an alternative screening
procedure to avoid exposure to screening equipment.

Passengers who require medication during the flight should carry all medication in their
carry-on baggage. Liquid medications exceeding 100 ml are permitted in the cabin provided
a valid prescription or doctor's letter is presented at security. It is the passenger's
responsibility to ensure they carry sufficient medication for the journey including any
potential delays.

## Traveling with Infants and Children

Infants under 2 years of age may travel on a parent or guardian's lap free of charge
on domestic flights. On international flights an infant fare of 10% of the adult base
fare applies. Each adult may travel with a maximum of one lap infant.

Infants and children up to 2 years of age may alternatively occupy their own seat if
a child safety seat approved for aviation use is provided by the parent. The safety
seat must be secured to the aircraft seat using the standard seatbelt. AeroNova staff
can advise on approved seat models.

Children aged 2 to 11 are charged a child fare which is typically 75% of the adult
base fare on the same booking. Children aged 12 and above are charged the full adult
fare. All children under 14 must be seated next to at least one accompanying adult.
AeroNova will not seat a child under 14 apart from their traveling adult regardless
of the fare class or seat selection made.

## Unaccompanied Minors

Children aged 5 to 14 traveling without an accompanying adult must use AeroNova's
unaccompanied minor service. This service is not available for children under 5.
Children aged 15 and above may travel alone as standard passengers.

The unaccompanied minor service costs $60 per flight segment and includes dedicated
supervision from check-in through to handover at the destination. A responsible adult
must accompany the child to the check-in desk and complete the unaccompanied minor
declaration form. At the destination a pre-authorized adult must be present to collect
the child and present valid photo identification.

Unaccompanied minors are not permitted to travel on the last flight of the day on any
route to ensure that alternative arrangements can be made in the event of a delay or
disruption. Unaccompanied minors must be booked on direct flights only. Connecting
itineraries are not permitted for unaccompanied minors.

## Pregnancy

Passengers who are pregnant may travel on AeroNova flights subject to the following
conditions. Up to and including 27 weeks of pregnancy no restrictions apply and no
medical documentation is required. Between 28 and 35 weeks a doctor's letter confirming
fitness to fly and the expected due date must be presented at check-in. Travel after
36 weeks of pregnancy is not permitted on any AeroNova flight.

These restrictions apply to single pregnancies. For multiple pregnancies the cutoff
for unrestricted travel is 24 weeks and travel after 32 weeks is not permitted.

## Allergies

Passengers with severe allergies should notify AeroNova at least 48 hours before
departure. AeroNova will make reasonable efforts to accommodate allergy requests
including avoiding the serving of specific allergens in the immediate rows around
the passenger. However AeroNova cannot guarantee an allergen-free environment on
board and passengers with life-threatening allergies should carry appropriate
medication including epinephrine auto-injectors at all times during travel.

AeroNova does not operate peanut-free flights but will refrain from distributing
peanut-based snacks in the rows immediately surrounding a passenger with a declared
peanut allergy when notified in advance.
